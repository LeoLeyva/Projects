Evil/Savage Patrick

Source

Origin:

This meme originated from a scene in the Spongebob episode of "Nature Pants," in which Spongebob leaves his everyday life to live the life of jellyfish. In this particular scene, Patrick surprises Spongebob from above and attempts to catch him through a body slam.

Applicability:

This meme is typically utilized to express that one is in the mood for hell-raising and being "evil."

Krusty Krab vs. Chum Bucket

Source

Origin:

This meme is a part of a meme series in which two things are compared to one another through the use of the two rivaling restaurants in Spongebob: the Chum Bucket and the Krusty Krabs.

Applicability:

This meme is typically utilized to prove a point as to how one thing-- typically what is captioned/inserted in the Krusty Krabs part-- is significantly superior to the other.

"Do y'all hear sumn?"

Source

Origin:

This meme is actually part a series of memes in which various Spongebob characters are taken out of context and captioned with, "Do y'all hear sumn?" The pictures in this meme series that are used are those in which a character appears to be indifferent.

Applicability:

This meme is typically utilized to express disinterest and/or indifference towards the captioned action.

Tired Spongebob

Source

Origin:

This meme originated from a scene in the Spongebob episode of "Nature Pants," in which Spongebob vows to live the simple life of a Jellyfish and leaves his old life and friends. He is shown to be tired because he had to avoid Patrick and Sandy from capturing him and returning him back to his old life.

Applicability:

This meme is typically utilized to express absolute exhaustion when anything in life takes up more energy than necessary, or when life in general is simply overwhelming.
