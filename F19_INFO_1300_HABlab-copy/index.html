About

The Human and Animal Bone Lab(HABLab) is a powerful research coalition centered on the advanced analysis of osteological resources in two distinct but overlapping fields of inquiry: zooarchaeology (the analysis of animal remains) and bioarchaeology(the study of human remains in their archaeological context). Located in the historic McGraw Hall on Cornellâ€™s Arts Quad, our collaborative suite (McGraw 101) is comprised of a Zooarchaeology Research Laboratory (Director: Nerissa Russell), Bioarchaeology Research Laboratory (Director: Matthew Velasco), and a shared teaching space which hosts a rotating curriculum of courses centered on skeletal analysis and interpretation. Our research labs also provide opportunities for graduate and undergraduate students to gain hands-on experience in osteological curation and to pursue their own research projects using archival data, modern anatomical collections, and archaeological materials housed in our facilities.
