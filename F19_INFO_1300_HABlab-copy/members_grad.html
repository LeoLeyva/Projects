Students

srd97@cornell.edu

Samuel R. Disotell is a current MA student in the Cornell Institute of Archaeology and Material Studies. He specializes in social zooarchaeology and human-animal relationships. His current work has him identifying and analyzing the faunal remains from the Historic Period Seneca site of White Springs (1688-1715). His MA thesis, currently in progress, focuses on the local deer hide and beaver fur trade, as well as Seneca adaptation, resilience, and maintenance of traditional lifeways.

afw53@cornell.edu

Anna Whittemore entered the Anthropology PhD program in the in the Fall of 2019. Her research will use bioarchaeological methods in order to characterize lifeways, including health, violence, and patterned migration in the Peruvian Andes during the formation of the Inka empire. She specializes in the analysis of light stable isotopes as indicators of diet and mobility in ancient populations.
