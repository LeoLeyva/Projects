Proyecto Collaguas Antiguos (Peru)

Proyecto de Colecciones Museográficos “Collaguas Antiguos” (PCA) investigates how social and ethnic identity shaped health outcomes during a period of intensive conflict and ecological stress in the late prehispanic highland Andes (AD 1100-1450). During the 2018 field season, project members collected data on skeletal lesions that indicate childhood stress and generated a comprehensive inventory of dental remains, recording patterns of dental caries, dental wear, and antemortem tooth loss, among other variables. This analysis will allow for the examination of sex and status-based differences in dental health and dietary behavior in the late prehispanic Colca Valley. Student volunteers on the project and in the HABLab have participated in all phases of data collection and analysis, including the dissemination of research results at regional and national conferences.
